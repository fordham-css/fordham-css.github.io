Computer Imaging Workshop

Curious about what kind of dog you are? Or about how old you and your friends look in pictures? Ever wonder how these kinds of sites can interpret pictures?

In this workshop, we'll be using Microsoft’s Project Oxford API to do facial and emotion recognition on pictures and put together our very own fun websites! (Check out the API here! https://www.projectoxford.ai/)

This workshop will be hosted by Microsoft and FREE PIZZA will be provided! What better way to spend your Wednesday night than by building the next best side project on your resume?!

Before attending, please try to have the following programs and accounts created:
1. Download Node.js: https://nodejs.org/en/download/
2. Install GraphicsMagick (or Image Magick; GraphicsMagick is easier to set up on Windows)
- Windows: go to http://www.graphicsmagick.org/download.html, click on Sourceforge and select the link next to "Looking for the latest version?"
- Mac: brew install graphicsmagick
3. Create a Microsoft account, if you don’t already have one.

Bring your friends and laptops! We'll see you there! Come for the pizza, #STAYFORTHECODE.

bitcamp

Fordham University's Computer Science Society is proud to have attended Bitcamp, a 36 hour hackathon experience at the University of Maryland!

Rewind, what's a hackathon?

It's an awesome 24-hour coding marathon. It's a great time to get together and collaborate (or not) with other hackers to build something awesome. If you're intimidated by the label "hacker"- don't be. You don't have to be a coding ninja to go to this thing. You just have to be excited and willing to give your best shot at making something cool or simply learning something new.

Read this quick article for a great summary of what goes on at hackathons.

ATTENDANCE IS COMPLETELY FREE AND ALL MEALS ARE PROVIDED! All you have to do is bring a laptop and charger and you're set! You can also show up any point during the day to take part. If you can't make it, we'll be sure to provide the stream for the demos at the end so you can cheer us on from home.

Visit our facebook page here: https://www.facebook.com/events/922996011066383
