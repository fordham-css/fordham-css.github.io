Coding Nights

Our weekly open coding sessions are the heart and soul of CSS
Wednesdays @ 7pm | Dealy 115

Computing Sciences Society's open coding nights are casual sessions geared towards collaborative working and helping each other out when we get stuck. We're here to help you leverage the technology out there to make your ideas come to life. Of course, you can also use the awesome space as a great environment for a personal project, classwork, or self-study Feel free to come and go as you please, but be prepared to leave with some (epic) stickers and more than a few inspired ideas.

bitcamp

Fordham University's Computing Sciences Society is proud to take you to bitcamp, a 36 hour hackathon experience at the University of Maryland!

Rewind, what's a hackathon?

It's an awesome 24-hour coding marathon. It's a great time to get together and collaborate (or not) with other hackers to build something awesome. If you're intimidated by the label "hacker"- don't be. You don't have to be a coding ninja to go to this thing. You just have to be excited and willing to give your best shot at making something cool or simply learning something new.

Read this quick article for a great summary of what goes on at hackathons.

ATTENDANCE IS COMPLETELY FREE AND ALL MEALS ARE PROVIDED! All you have to do is bring a laptop and charger and you're set! You can also show up any point during the day to take part. If you can't make it, we'll be sure to provide the stream for the demos at the end so you can cheer us on from home.
